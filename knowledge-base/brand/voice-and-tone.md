# Voice & Tone -- How We Sound

> **Last updated:** 2026-02-09
> **Source:** AI-KIP Tone of Voice Table (76 entries from real sales conversations)

---

## Overview

AI-KIP sounds like an experienced, confident founder explaining in a personal conversation -- not like a marketing department reading slides. We are consultative, challenging, practical, and future-oriented. We speak as equals and use our counterpart's language.

---

## The 7 Pillars of Our Voice

### 1. Consultative
We explain the "why" behind features and recommendations. We don't sell -- we educate.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Educate prospects on the reasoning behind features | Push features without context |
| Explain methodology and thinking | Present results without derivation |

> "At the end of the day, it's all about how we as humans actually operate together with intelligence."

### 2. Confident
We make direct claims backed by concrete results. No softeners, no hedging.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| State results confidently with specific numbers | Be vague or hedge unnecessarily |
| "We see an uplift from 300 to 500%" | "We could potentially see some improvement" |

### 3. Practical
We show, rather than tell. Live demos and real data over PowerPoint theory.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Show live demos with real data | Rely only on slides or theory |
| Work with the prospect's own data | Only show generic examples |

> "So let me switch here so this becomes more... here we have an overview of about 90,000 companies."

### 4. Future-Oriented
We frame our product as the inevitable market direction. Those who don't participate will fall behind.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Position as essential for future success | Focus only on current problems |
| Name timeframes (12-24 months) | Resort to apocalyptic fear-mongering |

> "This is going to happen in the next 12 to 24 months. Every organization who will survive this transition will use these kinds of toolings."

### 5. Simplifying
Reduce complex concepts to simple sentences that the prospect can repeat to their boss.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Create simple soundbites | Use jargon like "Agentic CDP" or "Fourth Pillar" |
| "We have customers. Find us more customers like our customers. How? Agents search the whole world." | Explain technical architecture in the first conversation |

### 6. Mutual Learning
We position ourselves as learners, not know-it-alls. Genuine curiosity about the other person's approach.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Show genuine interest in the prospect's setup | Act as all-knowing expert |
| "I would really love to learn also from you" | Deliver monologues |

### 7. Challenging with Care
We ask tough, probing questions -- but simultaneously acknowledge the other person's success.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Challenge assumptions directly but acknowledge success | Be confrontational, judgmental, or beat around the bush |
| "3.5 calls per day? Why? What are they doing the other 7 hours?... You're doing amazing as a company." | "That's way too few" or not address it at all |

## Language & Register

### Bilingual Flexibility
AI-KIP communicates fluently in German and English. We switch naturally between both languages depending on the counterpart's preference.

| Context | Language |
|---------|----------|
| DACH prospects (first contact) | German preferred, English if needed |
| Benelux prospects | English (or Dutch) |
| Marketing content DACH | German |
| Marketing content international | English |
| Technical documentation | English |
| Sales decks | Adapted to prospect |

### Register
- **First contact:** Professional but not stiff. Neither too formal ("Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren") nor too casual ("Hey!").
- **After getting acquainted:** More direct, more personal. "Du" when offered, "Sie" as default in German.
- **Content / Social media:** Approachable, opinionated, specific.

---

## Partnership Language

We deliberately use "we" and "together" phrasings. We position ourselves as collaboration partners, not vendors.

| Instead of | Better |
|-----------|--------|
| "Our product delivers to you..." | "Together we can..." |
| "As your vendor..." | "As your partner..." |
| "We sell..." | "We work together on..." |

---

## Boss-Ready Pitch

Every message must be simple enough that the prospect can explain it to their boss in one sentence.

**Template:**
> "We have customers. [AI-KIP] finds us more customers like our customers. How? AI agents search the whole world for similar companies and buying signals."

## Regional Adaptation

### German Market (DACH)
- Indirect approach preferred
- GDPR sensitivity is high -- always address proactively
- Data privacy materials expected in German
- Compliance first, then features

### Benelux Market
- More direct approach acceptable
- Less formal
- Faster decision cycles

## Objection Handling -- How We Respond to Objections

### "What about competitors?"
> "We will not work for your direct competitors. Nine times out of ten we say no."
Offer contractual exclusivity. Take the concern seriously.

### "Isn't this surveillance?"
> "The system mirrors insights to the employee themselves -- not to the head of sales. It helps that person get to the sale faster."
Reframe: Empowerment, not surveillance.

### "Why should we do this now?"
> "In 12 to 24 months, every organization that survives this transition will use these kinds of tools."
Frame as competitive necessity, not fear-mongering.

### "We've had bad experiences with AI before."
> "95% of real world tasks given to AI are failing -- because they used ChatGPT and other generic LLMs to do complex tasks. We work with specialized agent chains."
Explain the difference between generic AI and specialized agents.

### "Where is the data hosted?"
> "Everything is hosted here. Schwarz IT and StackIT. German tech stack. We can put it locally on a machine here in your headquarters if needed."
Name the specific German/European providers. Offer on-premise.

### "Does this work for small companies too?"
> "We understand per customer what the potential value is. We can find the right audiences for you also for the long tail -- craftsmen, one or two person shops."

### "Where does the data come from?"
> "Chamber of commerce, Dun & Bradstreet, Google Maps verified... everywhere there is a register we pull in that data."
Be transparent and specific. No evasive answers.

### "Isn't the personalization creepy?"
> "You have a scale of creepy versus cool. We're aware of that and adjust the approach."
Show awareness of boundaries rather than pushing aggressive personalization.

## Content Creation Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

- [ ] Does it sound like an experienced founder in conversation -- or like a generic marketing department?
- [ ] Are there concrete numbers and examples instead of vague promises?
- [ ] Can a prospect explain the core point to their boss in one sentence?
- [ ] Are we using "we"/"together" language instead of vendor language?
- [ ] Have we considered regional adaptations (DACH vs. Benelux)?
- [ ] Have we proactively addressed GDPR/hosting (not waited for objections)?
- [ ] Is the language simple enough? No jargon like "Agentic CDP" or "Fourth Pillar" in first contact?
- [ ] Are we positioning as empowerment, not surveillance/replacement?
