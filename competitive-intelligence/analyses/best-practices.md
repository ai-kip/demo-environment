# Best Practices -- Learnings from Competitor Analysis

> Last updated: 2026-02-09

---

## Overview

This document distills the most effective marketing tactics, strategies, and approaches observed across all 7 analyzed competitors. Each practice is ranked by impact, applicability, and difficulty. The goal is to create an actionable playbook of proven approaches that AI-KIP can adopt or adapt.

### 1. Category Creation -- Define the Market Vocabulary

**Observed at:** 6sense ("Dark Funnel," "Revenue AI"), Clay ("GTM Engineer," "Waterfall Enrichment"), 11x ("Digital Workers"), Outreach ("Sales Execution Gap")

**What it is:** Inventing proprietary terms and concepts that become the language buyers use to think about the problem space. The company that defines the vocabulary defines the market.

**Why it works:**
- Forces competitors to use YOUR framing (or explicitly argue against it)
- Creates top-of-mind association between the concept and your brand
- Enables thought leadership that cannot be commoditized
- Shifts buyer conversations from feature comparison to conceptual alignment

**Examples:**
| Company | Term Created | Impact |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| 6sense | "Dark Funnel" | Created urgency around invisible buyer behavior; entire content library built around it |
| Clay | "GTM Engineer" | Created a professional identity; users adopt the title and evangelize Clay to validate it |
| 11x | "Digital Workers" | Reframed AI agents as employees, changing the buying conversation from "tool" to "hiring" |
| Outreach | "Sales Execution Gap" | Named the problem, positioning Outreach as the solution to a gap nobody else had defined |

**How to apply:** Identify the core problem AI-KIP solves and name it. Create a term that buyers will start using in their own conversations. Build content around the concept before naming the product as the solution.

**Difficulty:** Medium | **Impact:** Very High | **Time to Results:** 6-18 months

### 2. Dogfooding as the Ultimate Marketing Story

**Observed at:** 6sense ("6sense for 6sense"), 11x (Alice generates their own leads), Clay (uses own product for GTM)

**What it is:** Using your own product as your primary go-to-market tool, then transparently sharing the results as marketing content.

**Why it works:**
- Creates the most authentic case study possible -- you can't fake your own results
- Generates continuous, fresh proof points without needing customer permission
- Creates a growth flywheel: product improvement → better own results → stronger marketing story → more customers → more data → product improvement
- Builds trust ("if they use it themselves, it must work")

**Best Example:** 6sense's "6sense for 6sense" content series transparently shows how they use their own platform for pipeline generation, ABM campaigns, and demand generation. It simultaneously serves as a product demo, case study, and thought leadership piece.

**How to apply:** Use AI-KIP for your own marketing intelligence. Document and publish the results. Create a recurring content series showing real insights derived from your own platform.

**Difficulty:** Low | **Impact:** Very High | **Time to Results:** Immediate

### 3. Founder-Led LinkedIn as Primary Early-Stage Channel

**Observed at:** 42db (dual-founder strategy), Venta AI (Lucas Spreiter), 11x (Hasan Sukkar pre-controversy), Clay (early days)

**What it is:** Founders personally posting thought leadership content on LinkedIn, building personal credibility that transfers to the company brand.

**Why it works:**
- Zero cost, immediate activation
- LinkedIn's algorithm favors personal profiles over company pages (2-5x more reach)
- Personal stories and opinions generate higher engagement than corporate content
- Builds trust through authenticity and vulnerability
- Creates a direct relationship between the market and the founding team

**Best Example:** 42db's dual-founder approach is the most sophisticated version: Julius Betzler (tech/product perspective) and Timo Trunk (sales/GTM perspective) post from their respective domains, creating a two-pronged credibility attack that covers both the product and the buyer's reality.

**How to apply:**
1. Post 3-5x per week on LinkedIn from personal founder accounts
2. Split content into lanes: one founder covers product/technology, one covers sales/market insights
3. Mix formats: hot takes (opinions), stories (lessons learned), data (insights from the product), frameworks (how-tos)
4. Be opinionated -- the most engaging founder content takes a clear stance

**Difficulty:** Low | **Impact:** High | **Time to Results:** 2-4 weeks

### 4. Community as a Growth Moat

**Observed at:** Clay (12K-15K Slack members), Outreach (56K Sales Hacker members), 6sense (RevCity), Luna.ai (community forum)

**What it is:** Building an owned community platform where users help each other, share strategies, and build relationships around the product and its category.

**Why it works:**
- Creates switching costs (social network effects -- people stay for the community, not just the product)
- Reduces support costs (peer-to-peer help)
- Generates product feedback and feature requests
- Produces endless user-generated content
- Creates word-of-mouth amplification
- Compounds over time (Clay's community took 7 years to reach 15K but now drives compounding returns)

**Best Example:** Clay's Slack community is the gold standard. Started in 2017 with a handful of members, it now has 12K-15K members and 70+ enterprise channels. Users share workflows, troubleshoot problems, and evangelize Clay to their networks. The community IS the product's growth engine.

**Shortcut Example:** Outreach acquired Sales Hacker (56K members) in 2018 instead of building from scratch -- a media property acquisition that provided instant community, content, and credibility.

**How to apply:**
1. Launch a focused Slack or Discord group for your ICP (start with 20-50 hand-picked members)
2. Seed it with valuable content and actively participate
3. Create distinct channels for topics your audience cares about
4. Be patient -- community growth is slow at first but compounds
5. Consider hosting weekly office hours or Q&A sessions to drive engagement

**Difficulty:** Medium | **Impact:** Very High | **Time to Results:** 6-24 months (compounding)

### 5. Programmatic SEO at Scale

**Observed at:** Clay (glossary, dossier pages, free tools), 11x (competitor alternative pages), 6sense (glossary, comparison pages), Outreach (educational content, comparison pages)

**What it is:** Creating hundreds or thousands of templated, data-driven pages that target long-tail keywords at scale.

**Why it works:**
- Captures high-intent, bottom-of-funnel search traffic ("X alternatives," "What is Y")
- Scales efficiently -- once the template is built, adding pages is low-cost
- When built on proprietary data, creates a moat competitors cannot replicate
- Compounds over time as domain authority grows

**Three Proven Models:**
1. **Glossary/Definition Pages (Clay, 6sense):** "What is [term]?" pages for industry terminology. 100+ pages targeting top-of-funnel educational keywords.
2. **Competitor Alternative Pages (11x, Luna.ai):** "Top 10 [Competitor] Alternatives" pages targeting high-purchase-intent keywords. Each page positions your product as the top alternative.
3. **Company Dossier Pages (Clay):** Thousands of company profile pages built from enrichment data. Captures search traffic from people researching specific companies.

**How to apply:**
1. Start with 20-30 glossary pages for key terms in your category
2. Create competitor alternative pages for 10-15 named competitors
3. If you have proprietary data (company intelligence, marketing benchmarks), turn it into public pages
4. Use a consistent template for each page type to enable rapid scaling

**Difficulty:** Medium | **Impact:** Very High | **Time to Results:** 3-12 months

### 6. Product-Led Growth with Instant Time-to-Value

**Observed at:** Clay (free plan + 14-day trial), Luna.ai (free plan, AI chatbot onboarding), 6sense (50 free credits)

**What it is:** Letting the product itself serve as the primary marketing and conversion channel. Users experience value before paying.

**Why it works:**
- Eliminates friction in the buyer journey
- Creates a self-serve pipeline that scales without sales headcount
- Word-of-mouth amplification (users share discoveries with colleagues)
- Product usage data informs marketing and product development
- The companies with the fastest growth (Clay: $1M→$100M ARR in 2 years) all have PLG motions

**Best Example:** Luna.ai's AI chatbot onboarding immediately asks about your business, starts suggesting leads, and delivers value within the first session -- before the user even finishes setup. This near-instant time-to-value converts free users to paid at high rates.

**Key Metrics from Competitors:**
- Luna.ai: 80% of AI-suggested leads approved by users
- Clay: Free plan (100 credits/mo) → 14-day Pro trial → Self-serve purchase
- 6sense: 50 free data credits/month as a low-friction entry point

**How to apply:**
1. Offer a free tier or generous trial (14 days, no credit card)
2. Design onboarding to deliver value within the first 5 minutes
3. Track time-to-first-value as a key product metric
4. Use in-product nudges to guide users from free to paid

**Difficulty:** High (requires product investment) | **Impact:** Very High | **Time to Results:** 3-6 months post-launch

### 7. Content Repurposing -- Maximum ROI from Every Piece

**Observed at:** 6sense (webinars → blog → LinkedIn → YouTube), Outreach (conference keynotes → on-demand → blog → podcast → social)

**What it is:** Systematically transforming every content piece into multiple formats across channels, maximizing reach and ROI from a single investment.

**Why it works:**
- Multiplies the value of each content investment by 5-10x
- Reaches different audiences who prefer different formats
- Creates consistent messaging across all channels
- Enables high publishing frequency with lean teams

**6sense Repurposing Chain:**
```
Webinar Recording
  → Blog Post (key takeaways)
  → LinkedIn Post (key insight)
  → YouTube Video (full recording)
  → YouTube Clip (highlight)
  → Podcast Episode (audio extract)
  → Email Newsletter (summary + CTA)
```

**Outreach Repurposing Chain:**
```
Unleash Conference Keynote
  → On-Demand Video
  → Blog Post (key insights)
  → Sales Hacker Article
  → Podcast Discussion
  → Social Media Clips
  → Data Report (if data-driven)
```

**How to apply:**
1. Start with one "anchor" format (webinar, interview, or long-form post)
2. Extract 3-5 derivative pieces from each anchor
3. Distribute across channels over 2-4 weeks (don't publish everything at once)
4. Prioritize: LinkedIn post, blog article, email newsletter as minimum derivatives

**Difficulty:** Low | **Impact:** High | **Time to Results:** Immediate

### 8. Creator/Ecosystem-Driven Distribution

**Observed at:** Clay (200-300+ creators, Limelight-managed), Luna.ai (20% lifetime affiliate commission)

**What it is:** Building a network of external content creators, affiliates, and partners who produce content about your product in exchange for revenue share, recognition, or early access.

**Why it works:**
- Creates authentic, high-volume content at near-zero cost to the company
- Content feels organic because it IS organic -- real people sharing real experiences
- Scales beyond what any internal marketing team can produce
- Each creator brings their own audience
- Creates social proof through sheer volume of mentions

**Clay's Tiered Ecosystem:**
```
Creator (entry) → Expert → Enterprise Partner
  200-300+ active creators
  $50 per paid referral OR 20% revenue share for first year
  Managed via Limelight platform
  Yarn video tool for branded content creation
```

**How to apply:**
1. Start with 5-10 power users who genuinely love the product
2. Offer them incentives: revenue share (15-20%), early access, co-marketing
3. Make it easy: provide templates, brand assets, talking points
4. Showcase their content on your company channels (reposting, featuring)
5. Scale gradually -- quality of creators matters more than quantity early on

**Difficulty:** Medium-High | **Impact:** Very High | **Time to Results:** 6-12 months

### 9. Compliance/Regulation as a Competitive Moat

**Observed at:** Venta AI (GDPR-first architecture), 42db (European-first positioning)

**What it is:** Turning regulatory compliance from a cost center into a competitive advantage, especially in markets where US-centric competitors struggle.

**Why it works:**
- Creates a genuine trust advantage in regulated markets (especially EU/DACH)
- US competitors must retrofit compliance; European-native companies have it built in
- "Made in Europe" / "GDPR-compliant" serves as both a trust signal and a differentiator
- Legal restrictions on cold email in Europe make compliance a functional necessity, not just a checkbox

**Best Example:** Venta AI turned GDPR from a limitation into their core positioning: "The compliant AI colleague for sales teams in Europe." Their data is EU-hosted, their AI is trained on German-language data, and they offer legally compliant channels (handwritten letters) where cold email is illegal.

**How to apply:**
1. If targeting European markets, lead with compliance credentials (GDPR, UWG, ePrivacy)
2. Position compliance as a feature, not a limitation ("built for Europe, not retrofitted")
3. Create content around compliance topics (these are underserved keywords)
4. Use "hosted in EU" and "data stays in Europe" as explicit trust signals

**Difficulty:** Medium | **Impact:** High (in European markets) | **Time to Results:** Immediate

### 10. Strategic Repositioning -- Evolving with the Market

**Observed at:** 6sense (ABM Tool → Revenue AI Platform), Outreach (Sales Engagement → Sales Execution → AI Revenue Workflow), Clay (Spreadsheets → Data Enrichment → GTM Engineering)

**What it is:** Deliberately and progressively evolving positioning to expand the addressable market and elevate the buyer persona.

**Why it works:**
- Each repositioning expands TAM and attracts new buyer segments
- Timed to market trends (AI, revenue operations, etc.)
- Elevates conversations from tactical to strategic, opening C-suite conversations
- Prevents being boxed into a narrow category

**Evolution Patterns:**

| Company | Phase 1 | Phase 2 | Phase 3 |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| 6sense | ABM Tool | Revenue Intelligence | Revenue AI Platform |
| Outreach | Sales Engagement | Sales Execution | AI Revenue Workflow |
| Clay | Spreadsheets | Data Enrichment | GTM Engineering Platform |
| 11x | AI SDR | Digital Workers | Platform X |

**Key Insight:** The most successful repositionings are:
- Timed to market shifts (AI hype cycle, revenue operations movement)
- Expansive (broader category, not narrower)
- Backed by product evolution (not just messaging changes)
- Gradual (each shift builds on the last)

**How to apply:**
1. Document your current positioning explicitly
2. Test 5-10 alternative positioning statements with prospects
3. Be willing to iterate -- Clay tested 25+ positioning statements
4. Plan positioning evolution in advance: where does the market need you to be in 12-18 months?

**Difficulty:** Medium | **Impact:** Very High | **Time to Results:** 3-12 months per shift

### 11. Owned Events as Brand Amplifiers

**Observed at:** 6sense (Breakthrough, 1,300+ attendees), Outreach (Unleash, multi-day), Clay (Sculpt, 1st edition)

**What it is:** Running a proprietary conference or event that serves as the annual centerpiece for product announcements, community building, and brand reinforcement.

**Why it works:**
- Creates a concentrated moment of attention and FOMO
- Product announcements get maximum impact
- On-demand replay extends content lifecycle for months
- Community belonging and brand loyalty strengthen
- Generates press coverage and social media content

**Scaling Path:**
1. Start with small roundtables/dinners (5-20 people) -- builds personal relationships
2. Host webinar series (50-200 attendees) -- builds audience and content
3. Launch a single-day event (100-300 attendees) -- tests event operations
4. Scale to multi-day conference (500+ attendees) -- full brand event

**How to apply at early stage:**
- Host intimate dinners or roundtables in your target city (Munich, Berlin)
- Co-host meetups with complementary companies
- Run a monthly virtual event (webinar, fireside chat)

**Difficulty:** High (at scale) | **Impact:** Very High | **Time to Results:** 12+ months

### 12. "AI Employee" / Anthropomorphization Framing

**Observed at:** 11x (Alice, Julian -- "Digital Workers"), Venta AI ("AI colleague"), Luna.ai ("AI team member"), Clay ("GTM Engineer")

**What it is:** Giving AI products human names, roles, and identities rather than treating them as software tools.

**Why it works:**
- Changes the buying conversation: "Should we hire this AI colleague?" vs. "Should we buy this tool?"
- Makes the value proposition tangible and relatable
- At EUR 600-950/month, an "AI employee" feels like a bargain compared to a human SDR (EUR 3,000-5,000/month)
- Creates emotional connection with the product
- Makes pricing justification easier (headcount replacement ROI)

**Warning:** Over-claiming (as 11x learned) can backfire. The AI must deliver on the "employee" promise, or credibility suffers.

**How to apply:**
1. Consider naming your AI agent(s) -- give them identity
2. Frame pricing relative to human headcount cost (not software cost)
3. Use employee/colleague language in marketing ("hire," "onboard," "train")
4. But be honest about capabilities -- underpromise and overdeliver

**Difficulty:** Low | **Impact:** Medium-High | **Time to Results:** Immediate

### 13. VC/Investor Branding as Social Proof

**Observed at:** 11x (a16z, Benchmark logos prominently displayed), Venta AI (YC S23 badge in LinkedIn name), Clay (CapitalG, Sequoia)

**What it is:** Prominently featuring prestigious investor logos, accelerator badges, and funding milestones as trust signals.

**Why it works:**
- In emerging categories, buyers need trust signals before evaluating the product
- Prestigious VCs (a16z, YC, Sequoia) carry instant credibility
- Funding announcements generate earned media coverage
- Each funding round becomes a marketing event

**How to apply:**
1. If you have recognized investors/accelerators, feature them prominently on homepage, LinkedIn, and all materials
2. Treat each funding round as a marketing campaign (coordinated PR, blog post, social media)
3. Even angel investors with recognizable names add credibility

**Difficulty:** Low (if applicable) | **Impact:** Medium-High | **Time to Results:** Immediate

### 14. Competitive Comparison Pages

**Observed at:** 6sense (vs. ZoomInfo, vs. Demandbase), 11x (vs. Artisan), Outreach (vs. Salesloft), Luna.ai (vs. Salesforge)

**What it is:** Creating dedicated landing pages that compare your product against specific competitors, targeting bottom-of-funnel search queries like "[Competitor] alternatives."

**Why it works:**
- Captures high-purchase-intent traffic (people actively evaluating solutions)
- Inserts your brand into competitor research journeys
- Provides a structured framework for differentiation
- Ranks for "alternatives" and "vs." keywords that have strong buying signals

**How to apply:**
1. Create pages for 10-15 competitors (even if they are much larger -- you capture the search traffic)
2. Be honest and fair in comparisons (buyers can spot unfair comparisons)
3. Focus on genuine differentiators, not vague claims
4. Include a comparison table, key differences, and a clear CTA

**Difficulty:** Low | **Impact:** High | **Time to Results:** 1-3 months

### 15. Data-Backed Thought Leadership

**Observed at:** Outreach ("Sales 2025 Data Report," 43.9M deals analyzed), 6sense ("The Science of B2B" research series), Clay (data-driven articles using proprietary datasets)

**What it is:** Using proprietary platform data to produce original research, benchmarks, and insights that competitors cannot replicate.

**Why it works:**
- Creates the most defensible form of content marketing
- Earns backlinks, press coverage, and social shares
- Establishes authority and trust
- Produces derivative content for months (blog posts, social clips, presentations)
- Unique data = unique insights = thought leadership

**How to apply:**
1. Identify unique data your platform generates (marketing patterns, channel performance, industry benchmarks)
2. Package insights into an annual or quarterly report
3. Create derivative content: blog posts, LinkedIn posts, infographics
4. Pitch findings to industry publications for earned media

**Difficulty:** Medium | **Impact:** High | **Time to Results:** 3-6 months

### 16. Free Tools as Lead Magnets and SEO Assets

**Observed at:** Outreach (ROI Calculator, Pipeline Calculator), Clay (CPM Calculator, Employee Count Finder), 6sense (Chrome Extension)

**What it is:** Offering standalone utility tools for free that provide immediate value while generating leads and earning backlinks.

**Why it works:**
- Delivers value before any purchase decision
- Earns backlinks naturally (people link to useful tools)
- Drives organic traffic for tool-related keywords
- Demonstrates product capabilities in a low-commitment way
- Captures leads (email for tool access)

**How to apply:**
1. Identify a small, useful calculation or lookup your audience needs
2. Build it as a standalone page on your website
3. Optimize for SEO ("B2B marketing ROI calculator," "sales intelligence cost calculator")
4. Gate minimally -- require only email, or make it completely open

**Difficulty:** Medium | **Impact:** Medium-High | **Time to Results:** 1-3 months

### 17. University / Academy Programs

**Observed at:** Clay (Clay University, modeled after Webflow University), Outreach (Outreach University)

**What it is:** A structured educational platform that teaches users how to use the product and succeed in their role, often with certifications.

**Why it works:**
- Drives product adoption and reduces churn
- Creates "product-educated" users who are harder to switch
- Certifications become career credentials, creating personal investment in the platform
- Serves as a marketing channel (prospects browse courses before buying)
- Reduces support burden

**How to apply:**
1. Start with 5-10 core lessons covering the most common use cases
2. Use video (screen recordings with voiceover are sufficient)
3. Add a certification badge for completion
4. Make it publicly accessible (not just for customers)

**Difficulty:** Medium | **Impact:** Medium-High | **Time to Results:** 3-6 months

### 18. Quantified Messaging Contrasts

**Observed at:** 42db ("Not 10,000 leads. The 50 who are actually buying."), Luna.ai ("<1% bounce rate, 6x better than average"), Outreach ("$765B in pipeline created")

**What it is:** Using specific, quantified contrasts to make positioning tangible and memorable.

**Why it works:**
- Numbers are concrete -- they stick in memory
- Contrasts create a before/after narrative
- Specificity signals credibility (vague claims feel untrustworthy)
- Makes abstract value propositions immediately tangible

**Examples:**
| Company | Quantified Contrast |
|---------|-------------------|
| 42db | "Not 10,000 leads. The 50 who are actually buying." |
| Luna.ai | "<1% bounce rate (6x better than market average)" |
| Outreach | "43.9M deals managed, $765B in pipeline" |
| Venta AI | "7,000 companies filtered to 700 relevant, 600 hours saved" |
| 11x | "One Alice = 11 human SDRs" |

**How to apply:**
1. Identify your core metric that matters to buyers (time saved, meetings booked, accuracy, etc.)
2. Quantify it specifically (not "more meetings" but "3x more qualified meetings")
3. Create a contrast (old way vs. new way, you vs. industry average)
4. Use the quantified contrast as a headline or tagline

**Difficulty:** Low | **Impact:** High | **Time to Results:** Immediate

### 19. Unconventional Channel Innovation

**Observed at:** Venta AI (handwritten letters as outreach channel), Clay (Yarn video tool for creators), Outreach (Sales Hacker media acquisition)

**What it is:** Finding or creating marketing channels that competitors don't use, giving you differentiated reach.

**Why it works:**
- Stands out in an increasingly noisy digital landscape
- Creates genuine differentiation that is hard to copy
- Often has higher engagement because it is unexpected
- Can unlock new buyer segments

**Examples:**
- **Venta AI's handwritten letters:** In markets where cold email is illegal (most of Europe), physical letters are the only scalable outreach option. Venta reports 2x reply rates vs. email.
- **Clay's Yarn tool:** Enables creators to submit a voice note and receive a branded product video, scaling video content production through the creator ecosystem.
- **Outreach's Sales Hacker acquisition:** Instead of building a media property, they bought one -- instant 56K community and content engine.

**How to apply:**
1. Map where your competitors are NOT
2. Experiment with one unconventional channel (physical mail, partnerships, events, video)
3. Measure ROI before scaling
4. Consider: what channel would your ICP least expect to find you in?

**Difficulty:** Varies | **Impact:** Medium-High | **Time to Results:** 1-6 months

### 20. Transparent Pivot / Vulnerability Storytelling

**Observed at:** Venta AI (public pivot from order.link), Luna.ai (AppSumo to sunset), Outreach (GroupTalent to Outreach)

**What it is:** Founders publicly sharing the company's struggles, pivots, and failures as part of the brand narrative.

**Why it works:**
- Creates authenticity that polished corporate messaging cannot match
- Resonates with founders and leaders who have experienced similar challenges
- Builds trust through vulnerability
- Humanizes the brand

**Best Example:** Venta AI's Lucas Spreiter publicly shared the painful pivot from order.link to Venta AI -- including losing customers and employees. This story appeared in deutsche-startups.de and on his LinkedIn, building empathy and credibility with the founder community.

**How to apply:**
1. Identify a genuine struggle or turning point in your company's journey
2. Share it authentically on LinkedIn and in press interviews
3. Connect the struggle to a lesson that benefits your audience
4. Don't over-engineer it -- raw authenticity outperforms polished narratives

**Difficulty:** Low | **Impact:** Medium | **Time to Results:** Immediate

## Competitive Advantage Summary

### What the Best Companies All Do
1. **Own their category vocabulary** (6sense, Clay, 11x, Outreach)
2. **Use their own product as marketing** (6sense, 11x, Clay)
3. **Invest in community** (Clay, Outreach, 6sense)
4. **Build SEO moats** (6sense, Clay, Outreach)
5. **Evolve positioning with the market** (6sense, Outreach, Clay)

### What No Competitor Does Well
1. **YouTube-native content** -- all use it as a dump, none as a primary channel
2. **German-language content marketing** -- massive gap despite DACH targeting
3. **DACH-focused community** -- no one owns this space
4. **Newsletter as standalone product** -- no high-value newsletter exists in the space
5. **Physical events in Europe** -- zero presence

### AI-KIP's Best Opportunities
1. **Own the European B2B Intelligence category** -- no competitor dominates here
2. **Build the DACH sales intelligence community first** -- first mover advantage
3. **Create German-language content at scale** -- biggest content gap in the market
4. **Combine compliance + intelligence** -- Venta AI does compliance, 6sense does intelligence, nobody does both well
5. **Launch a DACH-focused podcast/newsletter** -- uncontested niche
